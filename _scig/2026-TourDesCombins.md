The hike will lead us on the Swiss half of the Tour des Combins with
an ascent of Mont Rogneux. You need to be comfortable hiking on blue-white alpine trails (T4).

## Itinerary
<b>Day 1: {% include hikestats.html up="1100m" down="280m" distance="11km" duration="4h30m" %}</b><br/>
We take a train and bus to **Bourg-St-Pierre** and start the hike with the first stage of the 
[Tour des Combins](https://mongrandtourdescombins.com) reaching **[Cabane de Mille](https://www.cabanedemille.ch/)**. 
Along the way we will have dramatic views of the Mont Blanc range.

<b>Day 2: {% include hikestats.html up="1300m" down="1150m" distance="12km" duration="7h" %}</b><br/>
Today we will divert from the official Tour des Combins and instead take the adventurous "shortcut"
across **Mont Rogneux (3084m)**. This follows a blue-white alpine trail (T4) but does not pose any major
technical difficulties. We will be rewarded with a 360 degree panorama and another 3000m peak in your pocket.
We descend Mont Rogneux, ascend the **Col des Avouillons** and make our way to the 
**[Cabane FXB Panossière](https://panossiere.ch/en/)** at the base of the Grand Combins massif.

<b>Day 3: {% include hikestats.html up="400m" down="1000m" distance="8km" duration="4h" %}</b><br/>
We hike up to **Col des Otanes** (1h, 250m) from where we will have multiple options. The most direct way is
to descend to the **Mauvoisin dam** (2h30m, -1000m). A slightly longer alternative is to end in Fionnay.
If there is a motivated group and if conditions allow, there is also the option to scramble up to the
top of Grand Tavé (3158m, T4) from the col. Depending on the timing we may decide to call a private
taxi to bring us back to Le Châble.


## <i class="bi bi-card-checklist"></i>Equipment
- Usual hiking equipment including rain gear, warm layer and gloves.
- Hut sleeping bag (“sac à viande”) and toiletries.
- Minimum one picnic for the first day + snacks (extra picnics can also be bought in huts).
- Buy your one-way train ticket before departure.
- Optional:
  - Sandals or flip-flops for the train ride can be comfy (otherwise huts provide slippers).
  - Thermos bottle (huts usually provide tea for the day in the morning).
